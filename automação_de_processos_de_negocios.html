Business Process Automation

Overview

Business Process Automation (BPA) is the use of software to automate reproducible and multi-step business transactions. Compared to other types of automation, BPA solutions are often complex, connected to various enterprise IT systems, and customized specifically to the needs of organizations.
It is common for organizations to apply BPA in a digital transformation strategy to optimize workflows and increase operational efficiency.

What is the relationship between BPA and Business Process Management (BPM)?

Business Process Management (BPM) is the collaboration between IT and business teams to model, analyze, and optimize complete processes to help achieve strategic objectives. BPM is an ongoing process and brings improvements over time.
While BPM is a practice that considers the organization as a whole, BPA is a strategy to enhance specific processes.
BPM aims to eliminate ad-hoc workflow management practices and optimize business operations to deliver better services and solutions to customers.
BPA can be used independently or as part of a comprehensive BPM initiative.
Both enhance processes and tasks that are reproducible, continuous, or predictable. They increase efficiency and reduce costs and errors.
The combination of BPA and BPM can be powerful because BPM provides a detailed view of the architecture of all business processes to be mapped and automated. When applied in a BPM practice, BPA can be used to continuously monitor and improve process efficiency.
However, they can also work well separately. Decoupled from an integrated business automation solution, BPM and BPA function as independent initiatives to improve efficiency and reduce costs.

What is the relationship between BPA and Robotic Process Automation (RPA)?

In Robotic Process Automation (RPA), you use software to automate specific repetitive tasks. This software is trained to simulate repetitive human steps, such as copying and pasting data into a field.
BPA software often handles more complex tasks than RPA. BPA describes automated transaction steps at various stages that involve a series of business systems.
Many RPA solutions are pre-built, easy-to-install software tools. They run on existing systems without connecting to databases or accessing application programming interfaces (APIs).
BPA solutions are complex and customized according to the specific organization. They are usually integrated with data systems or connected to APIs. It is possible to incorporate certain RPA software into a BPA solution.

What is the best type of software for business process automation?

Companies always want to streamline operations and save costs. Therefore, they often turn to business process automation software to enhance specific areas. This includes input detection, decision augmentation and automation, and the use of automated responses.
The architecture of business automation software makes all the difference. It is recommended that these tools can run on different types of infrastructure, such as the edge where business decisions are made and scalable cloud environments according to demand. An increasingly used strategy is to separate the user interface from the workflows themselves, which are created as services called by APIs.
The solution includes various lightweight services that can be deployed in containers, such as Red Hat OpenShift, and embedded in custom solutions. These features provide scale and flexibility, making the solution the perfect choice for larger companies that need to automate many business decisions.
