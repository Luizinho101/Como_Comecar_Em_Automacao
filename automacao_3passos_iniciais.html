AUTOMATION: THE 3 INITIAL STEPS EVERYONE NEEDS TO KNOW

Have you noticed that automation is at the center of technology conversations? The topic is increasingly popular. Whether at international events or in prestigious reports, professionals in the field are becoming aware increasingly about the importance of automating and giving intelligence to processes to have more security and efficiency in decision making.

It turns out that it is often not very easy to understand the first steps on the journey towards a successful project. in the area of ​​automation. There are many doubts. But we guarantee: the important thing is to start. Even because a project automation never ends.

Like this? Does that mean you'll be redoing that same project forever? Yes and no. Automation within of technology is like a vital sign, and like any vital sign, it needs support to be maintained. After To begin with, maintaining work is essential to success.

Automation is a continuous process

Automation is the act of automating a process and giving intelligence to the system so that it can make decisions on its own based on of that event. This can range from a simple project to more complex ones.

To be clearer: automation works based on an automated process, but with some intelligence in the his decision making. This could be, for example, extracting a list of names from the customer register on a daily basis and put in a repository. This is automatic. Seeing and highlighting customers with specific names or ages is automation of this process.

Ok. But let's say you want to insert automation into your processes now. What are the first steps What everyone should know when starting out?

STEP 1: MAP THE PROCESSES.

First of all: for any automation, you need to have a very clear flow. It seems simple, but the best The thing to do is document every step of your work. This can be done in several ways, but ideally is to have most of the details described. Note carefully: the majority. We know that it is impossible to document all stages of any process.

So you might as well start small. And this also needs records. Define where the first project begins and how far it goes. This is part of our second tip.

STEP 2: SET STANDARDS AND SCHEDULES

Anything that is repetitive is easier to automate. And everything that is automated will be the first step towards automation. After mapping the processes, it will be easy to understand everything that is repeated. Very or little, it doesn't matter.

Standards. This part is very important.

Identify patterns in work and set timelines for implementing steps. Automation is a cycle. You will eventually need to go back to the first steps to make corrections and add intelligence, this It's part of your system training.

That's why it's important to have standards and processes well mapped out. And monitored. Which brings us to the third step.

STEP 3: MONITOR

Qualquer projeto de automação precisa ser monitorado. Um erro muito comum é o de acreditar que adicionar automação a um processo é um ato pontual, quando na verdade é um trabalho contínuo de monitoramento e melhorias.

Automation will make decisions and bring results to you. It is important that this action is monitored to constantly improve the functioning of your system.

In fact, a good strategy is to define from the beginning how your processes will be monitored. The risk of errors will be much smaller. And even if errors happen, they will be documented and visible, which will make it easier a lot to teach the system and have a more refined automation process.

Remember the list of names? In the beginning, the automation process may result in changed names, or end up selecting homonyms you didn't ask for. By monitoring these results, you will be able to adjust your project for better results. best.

Finally, always write everything down. You can't improve something that isn't measured.

So, did you like it? So let's start automating the first processes.
